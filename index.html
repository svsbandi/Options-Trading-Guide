Options Trading Strategies

Examples below use the Nifty 50 spot price of ₹24,813 (as of May 22, 2025). Each includes a payoff chart and detailed explanation. Lot size is 75 for Nifty 50 options.

Bullish Strategies (Expecting Price Increase)

Long Call

Bullish • Medium Risk

Buy a call option to profit from an expected rise in the underlying asset’s price.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one ATM call option with a strike price of ₹24,800, expiring in one month, for a premium of ₹200 (₹15,000 total).

Breakeven: ₹24,800 + ₹200 = ₹25,000

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Option expires worthless. Loss = ₹15,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,000: Breakeven. Payoff = (₹25,000 - ₹24,800 - ₹200) × 75 = ₹0.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Payoff = (₹25,500 - ₹24,800 - ₹200) × 75 = ₹37,500.

Explanation: The Long Call is ideal when you expect Nifty 50 to rise significantly. Your risk is limited to the premium paid (₹15,000). If Nifty exceeds ₹25,000, profits increase linearly with no upper limit. Time decay (theta) erodes the option’s value as expiration nears, so timing is critical.

Bull Call Spread

Bullish • Low Risk

Buy a call at a lower strike price and sell a call at a higher strike price.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and sell one call at ₹25,200 for ₹50 (₹3,750). Net cost = ₹150 × 75 = ₹11,250.

Breakeven: ₹24,800 + ₹150 = ₹24,950

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Both options expire worthless. Loss = ₹11,250.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,000: Payoff = (₹25,000 - ₹24,800 - ₹150) × 75 = ₹3,750.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Payoff = (₹25,200 - ₹24,800 - ₹150) × 75 = ₹18,750.

Explanation: The Bull Call Spread limits both risk and reward. Maximum loss is the net premium (₹11,250), and maximum profit is capped at ₹18,750 if Nifty exceeds ₹25,200. This strategy reduces the cost of a Long Call but caps upside potential.

Covered Call

Bullish • Medium Risk

Own the underlying stock and sell a call option to generate income.

Scenario: You own 75 shares of Nifty 50 ETF at ₹24,813 and sell one OTM call at ₹25,200 for ₹50 (₹3,750).

Breakeven: ₹24,813 - ₹50 = ₹24,763

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Stock value = ₹24,500 × 75 = ₹1,837,500. Loss = ₹1,837,500 - ₹1,861,125 + ₹3,750 = -₹19,875.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,200: Stock value = ₹1,890,000. Profit = ₹1,890,000 - ₹1,861,125 + ₹3,750 = ₹32,625.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Profit capped at ₹32,625 (same as ₹25,200 due to call assignment).

Explanation: The Covered Call generates income (₹3,750) but caps upside at ₹25,200. If Nifty falls, you face downside risk on the stock, offset slightly by the premium. Maximum profit is ₹32,625.

Synthetic Long Stock

Bullish • High Risk

Buy a call and sell a put at the same strike to mimic owning the stock.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and sell one put at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000). Net cost = ₹0.

Breakeven: ₹24,800

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Put payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,500) × 75 = -₹22,500. Loss = -₹22,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Both options expire worthless. Payoff = ₹0.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Call payoff = (₹25,500 - ₹24,800) × 75 = ₹52,500. Profit = ₹52,500.

Explanation: The Synthetic Long Stock mimics stock ownership without the capital outlay. Upside is unlimited, but downside risk is significant if Nifty falls below ₹24,800. No net cost due to equal premiums.

Cash-Secured Put

Bullish • Medium Risk

Sell a put option with cash reserved to buy the stock if assigned.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You sell one put at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) with cash reserved to buy at ₹24,800.

Breakeven: ₹24,800 - ₹200 = ₹24,600

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Put payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,500) × 75 = -₹22,500 + ₹15,000 = -₹7,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,600: Payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,600) × 75 = -₹15,000 + ₹15,000 = ₹0.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,000: Put expires worthless. Profit = ₹15,000.

Explanation: The Cash-Secured Put generates income (₹15,000) if Nifty stays above ₹24,800. If Nifty falls below ₹24,600, you start losing money, with losses increasing as Nifty drops further.

Bearish Strategies (Expecting Price Decrease)

Long Put

Bearish • Medium Risk

Buy a put option to profit from an expected decline in the underlying asset’s price.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one ATM put option with a strike price of ₹24,800, expiring in one month, for a premium of ₹200 (₹15,000 total).

Breakeven: ₹24,800 - ₹200 = ₹24,600

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,000: Option expires worthless. Loss = ₹15,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,600: Breakeven. Payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,600 - ₹200) × 75 = ₹0.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,000: Payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,000 - ₹200) × 75 = ₹45,000.

Explanation: The Long Put profits when Nifty 50 falls below the breakeven point (₹24,600). Risk is limited to the premium (₹15,000), while the maximum reward is significant but capped if Nifty reaches zero.

Bear Put Spread

Bearish • Low Risk

Buy a put at a higher strike and sell a put at a lower strike.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one put at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and sell one put at ₹24,400 for ₹50 (₹3,750). Net cost = ₹150 × 75 = ₹11,250.

Breakeven: ₹24,800 - ₹150 = ₹24,650

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,000: Both options expire worthless. Loss = ₹11,250.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,650: Payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,650 - ₹150) × 75 = ₹0.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,200: Payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,400 - ₹150) × 75 = ₹18,750.

Explanation: The Bear Put Spread limits both risk and reward. Maximum loss is the net premium (₹11,250), and maximum profit is capped at ₹18,750 if Nifty falls below ₹24,400.

Bear Call Spread

Bearish • Low Risk

Sell a call at a lower strike and buy a call at a higher strike.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You sell one call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and buy one call at ₹25,200 for ₹50 (₹3,750). Net credit = ₹150 × 75 = ₹11,250.

Breakeven: ₹24,800 + ₹150 = ₹24,950

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Both options expire worthless. Profit = ₹11,250.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,950: Payoff = ₹0.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Loss = (₹25,200 - ₹24,800 - ₹150) × 75 = -₹18,750.

Explanation: The Bear Call Spread profits if Nifty stays below ₹24,950. Maximum profit is the net credit (₹11,250), and maximum loss is ₹18,750 if Nifty exceeds ₹25,200.

Synthetic Short Stock

Bearish • High Risk

Buy a put and sell a call at the same strike to mimic shorting the stock.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one put at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and sell one call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000). Net cost = ₹0.

Breakeven: ₹24,800

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Put payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,500) × 75 = ₹22,500. Profit = ₹22,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Both options expire worthless. Payoff = ₹0.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Call payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹25,500) × 75 = -₹52,500. Loss = -₹52,500.

Explanation: The Synthetic Short Stock mimics shorting Nifty 50. Downside potential is significant if Nifty falls, but upside risk is unlimited if Nifty rises above ₹24,800.

Neutral Strategies (Expecting Little Price Movement)

Iron Condor

Neutral • Low Risk

Sell an out-of-the-money (OTM) call and put, buy a further OTM call and put.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You sell one OTM call (strike ₹25,200, premium ₹50), sell one OTM put (strike ₹24,400, premium ₹50), buy one further OTM call (strike ₹25,400, premium ₹30), and buy one further OTM put (strike ₹24,200, premium ₹30). Net credit = ₹40 × 75 = ₹3,000.

Breakeven: Upper: ₹25,200 + ₹40 = ₹25,240; Lower: ₹24,400 - ₹40 = ₹24,360

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Within range, keep full credit. Profit = ₹3,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,300: Loss = (₹25,300 - ₹25,200 - ₹40) × 75 = -₹4,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹23,800: Loss = (₹24,400 - ₹23,800 - ₹40) × 75 = -₹45,000 (max loss).

Explanation: The Iron Condor profits if Nifty stays between ₹24,360 and ₹25,240. Maximum profit is the net credit (₹3,000), and maximum loss is limited to ₹200 × 75 - ₹3,000 = ₹12,000.

Butterfly Spread

Neutral • Low Risk

Buy one call/put at a lower strike, sell two at a middle strike, buy one at a higher strike.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one call at ₹24,400 for ₹400 (₹30,000), sell two calls at ₹24,800 for ₹200 each (₹30,000 total), and buy one call at ₹25,200 for ₹50 (₹3,750). Net cost = ₹3,750.

Breakeven: Lower: ₹24,400 + ₹50 = ₹24,450; Upper: ₹25,200 - ₹50 = ₹25,150

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,400: Loss = ₹3,750.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Profit = (₹24,800 - ₹24,400 - ₹50) × 75 = ₹26,250.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,400: Loss = ₹3,750.

Explanation: The Butterfly Spread profits most if Nifty closes at ₹24,800. Maximum profit is ₹26,250, and maximum loss is the net premium (₹3,750). It’s a low-risk strategy for a specific price target.

Calendar Spread

Neutral • Medium Risk

Sell a short-term option and buy a longer-term option at the same strike.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You sell one call at ₹24,800 expiring in one month for ₹200 (₹15,000) and buy one call at ₹24,800 expiring in two months for ₹300 (₹22,500). Net cost = ₹100 × 75 = ₹7,500.

Breakeven: Varies based on volatility; approximately ₹24,900 at first expiration.

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Short call expires worthless, long call worth ~₹200. Loss = ₹7,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,000: Short call loss = (₹25,000 - ₹24,800) × 75 = ₹15,000; long call gain ~₹22,500. Net = ₹0.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,400: Profit ~₹7,500 (long call retains value).

Explanation: The Calendar Spread profits from time decay of the short-term option. Maximum loss is the net premium (₹7,500), and profit potential depends on volatility and time decay.

Straddle (Short)

Neutral • High Risk

Sell a call and put at the same strike, expecting minimal price movement.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You sell one call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and one put at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000). Net credit = ₹400 × 75 = ₹30,000.

Breakeven: Upper: ₹24,800 + ₹400 = ₹25,200; Lower: ₹24,800 - ₹400 = ₹24,400

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Both options expire worthless. Profit = ₹30,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Call loss = (₹25,500 - ₹24,800) × 75 = -₹52,500. Net = -₹22,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,000: Put loss = (₹24,800 - ₹24,000) × 75 = -₹60,000. Net = -₹30,000.

Explanation: The Short Straddle profits if Nifty stays near ₹24,800. Maximum profit is ₹30,000, but losses are unlimited if Nifty moves significantly in either direction.

Strangle (Short)

Neutral • High Risk

Sell an OTM call and OTM put, expecting the stock to stay within a range.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You sell one OTM call at ₹25,200 for ₹50 (₹3,750) and one OTM put at ₹24,400 for ₹50 (₹3,750). Net credit = ₹100 × 75 = ₹7,500.

Breakeven: Upper: ₹25,200 + ₹100 = ₹25,300; Lower: ₹24,400 - ₹100 = ₹24,300

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Both options expire worthless. Profit = ₹7,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Call loss = (₹25,500 - ₹25,200) × 75 = -₹22,500. Net = -₹15,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,000: Put loss = (₹24,400 - ₹24,000) × 75 = -₹30,000. Net = -₹22,500.

Explanation: The Short Strangle profits if Nifty stays between ₹24,300 and ₹25,300. Maximum profit is ₹7,500, but losses are unlimited if Nifty moves significantly.

Iron Butterfly

Neutral • Low Risk

Sell an at-the-money (ATM) call and put, buy an OTM call and put.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You sell one ATM call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000), sell one ATM put at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000), buy one OTM call at ₹25,200 for ₹50 (₹3,750), and buy one OTM put at ₹24,400 for ₹50 (₹3,750). Net credit = ₹300 × 75 = ₹22,500.

Breakeven: Upper: ₹24,800 + ₹300 = ₹25,100; Lower: ₹24,800 - ₹300 = ₹24,500

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Both sold options expire worthless. Profit = ₹22,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,300: Loss = (₹25,200 - ₹24,800 - ₹300) × 75 = -₹7,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,200: Loss = (₹24,800 - ₹24,400 - ₹300) × 75 = -₹7,500.

Explanation: The Iron Butterfly profits if Nifty stays near ₹24,800. Maximum profit is ₹22,500, and maximum loss is ₹7,500 if Nifty moves beyond ₹24,400 or ₹25,200.

Volatile Strategies (Expecting Large Price Movement)

Long Straddle

Volatile • Medium Risk

Buy a call and put at the same strike, expecting a significant price move in either direction.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one ATM call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and one ATM put at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000). Total cost = ₹400 × 75 = ₹30,000.

Breakeven: Upper: ₹24,800 + ₹400 = ₹25,200; Lower: ₹24,800 - ₹400 = ₹24,400

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Both options expire worthless. Loss = ₹30,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Call payoff = (₹25,500 - ₹24,800 - ₹400) × 75 = ₹22,500. Net profit = ₹22,500 - ₹15,000 = ₹7,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,000: Put payoff = (₹24,800 - ₹24,000 - ₹400) × 75 = ₹30,000. Net profit = ₹15,000.

Explanation: The Long Straddle profits from large price movements in either direction. Risk is limited to the premiums paid (₹30,000). The call side offers unlimited upside, while the put side’s reward is significant but limited if Nifty reaches zero.

Long Strangle

Volatile • Medium Risk

Buy an OTM call and OTM put, expecting a large price swing.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one OTM call at ₹25,200 for ₹50 (₹3,750) and one OTM put at ₹24,400 for ₹50 (₹3,750). Total cost = ₹100 × 75 = ₹7,500.

Breakeven: Upper: ₹25,200 + ₹100 = ₹25,300; Lower: ₹24,400 - ₹100 = ₹24,300

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Both options expire worthless. Loss = ₹7,500.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Call payoff = (₹25,500 - ₹25,200 - ₹100) × 75 = ₹15,000. Net profit = ₹15,000 - ₹3,750 = ₹11,250.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,000: Put payoff = (₹24,400 - ₹24,000 - ₹100) × 75 = ₹22,500. Net profit = ₹18,750.

Explanation: The Long Strangle is cheaper than a Long Straddle but requires a larger price move to profit. Risk is limited to ₹7,500, with unlimited upside on the call side and significant downside on the put side.

Reverse Iron Condor

Volatile • Low Risk

Buy an OTM call and put, sell a further OTM call and put, expecting a big move.

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one OTM call at ₹25,200 for ₹50 (₹3,750), buy one OTM put at ₹24,400 for ₹50 (₹3,750), sell one further OTM call at ₹25,400 for ₹30 (₹2,250), and sell one further OTM put at ₹24,200 for ₹30 (₹2,250). Net cost = ₹40 × 75 = ₹3,000.

Breakeven: Upper: ₹25,200 + ₹40 = ₹25,240; Lower: ₹24,400 - ₹40 = ₹24,360

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,800: Loss = ₹3,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,300: Profit = (₹25,400 - ₹25,200 - ₹40) × 75 = ₹12,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,000: Profit = (₹24,400 - ₹24,200 - ₹40) × 75 = ₹12,000.

Explanation: The Reverse Iron Condor profits from a large price move. Maximum profit is ₹12,000, and maximum loss is ₹3,000 if Nifty stays between ₹24,400 and ₹25,200.

Ratio Spread

Volatile • High Risk

Buy one option and sell multiple options at a different strike (e.g., buy 1 call, sell 2 calls).

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You buy one call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and sell two calls at ₹25,200 for ₹50 each (₹7,500 total). Net cost = ₹5,000.

Breakeven: ₹25,600 (complex due to ratio)

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Loss = ₹5,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,200: Profit = (₹25,200 - ₹24,800) × 75 - ₹5,000 = ₹25,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Loss = (₹25,200 - ₹24,800) × 75 - 2 × (₹25,500 - ₹25,200) × 75 - ₹5,000 = -₹2,500.

Explanation: The Ratio Spread profits if Nifty rises to ₹25,200 but can lead to unlimited losses if Nifty rises too far due to the extra short call. Maximum profit is ₹25,000, and risk increases above ₹25,600.

Backspread

Volatile • Medium Risk

Sell one option and buy multiple options at a different strike (e.g., sell 1 call, buy 2 calls).

Scenario: Nifty 50 is at ₹24,813. You sell one call at ₹24,800 for ₹200 (₹15,000) and buy two calls at ₹25,200 for ₹50 each (₹7,500 total). Net credit = ₹5,000.

Breakeven: ₹25,600 (complex due to ratio)

Payoff Calculation:

  • If Nifty closes at ₹24,500: Profit = ₹5,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,200: Loss = (₹25,200 - ₹24,800) × 75 - ₹5,000 = -₹25,000.
  • If Nifty closes at ₹25,500: Profit = 2 × (₹25,500 - ₹25,200) × 75 - (₹25,500 - ₹24,800) × 75 + ₹5,000 = ₹2,500.

Explanation: The Backspread profits from a large upward move, with unlimited potential above ₹25,600. Maximum loss is ₹25,000 if Nifty closes at ₹25,200, but risk is limited on the downside.

Notes

  • Risk Management: Always assess risk tolerance and use stop-loss or hedging techniques where applicable.
  • Commissions and Fees: Multiple-leg strategies (e.g., iron condor) may incur higher transaction costs.
  • Margin Requirements: Strategies like short straddles or synthetic positions may require significant margin.
  • Market Conditions: Choose strategies based on volatility, time to expiration, and market trends.
